Tech Blog Interview

By: Brendan DePuy

I decided to interview my mom because she uses technology for work almost everyday.

Brendan: How old were you got your first computer?

Mary: My brother was a freshman in college, when he got his first desktop. It was fall of 1991. I was a high school senior when I first got access to a computer at home. Since he lived at home, I was able to use it too. Before that, I had a word processor which was basically an electronic typewriter with limited memory.

Brendan: What was your favorite piece of technology when they were growing up?

Mary: My favorite piece of technology was my VCR. You could watch movies at home and you could tape things that are on TV. There was nothing worse than missing a good part of a show because you were in the bathroom.

Brendan: How has technology changed over your lifetime?

Mary: It has changed dramatically. I went from growing up in a house with a rotary phone to a touchtone phone. I knew the frustration of a busy signal. Then in law school, I had a pager which would let you know when someone was trying to get a hold of you. We went from there to flip phones to sidekicks, which were cellphones with larger keyboards for texting. Now, I carry around an iPhone, which is basically a mini computer, at all times.

Brendan: Have the changes you’ve seen in technology over your lifetime mostly helped or hurt society?

Mary: In some ways, they have helped, but in some ways they have hurt. On the one hand, it is great that you can always reach someone in case of emergency. Also, there have been great developments in the medical field by using technology. In addition, the internet is a valuable resource. On the other hand, the development of technology has weakened our social skills as a society. People don’t talk to each other as much, especially kids. My son plays video games with his friends online instead of seeing his friends in person. In addition, I far prefer reading a book as opposed to reading on a computer screen. Even at work, I do my legal research online instead of from books and my files are uploaded instead of paper files. Whie, I understand the environment is important, I find it easier to comprehend when I am reading when I am holding a paper. Finally, one huge downside of technology are sites like YouTube, which can have some very inappropriate content available to all.

Brendan: What is your greatest hope for technology in the future?

Mary: It can be used to solve problems, like our city’s crumbling infrastructure and it could be a great conduit for accurate information sharing.

Brendan: What is your greatest fear of technology in the future?

Mary: It will lead to the demise of social skills, interpersonal relationships, and communities in general. Also, as we have already seen with our elections, technology can be weaponized such as by cyber attacks and election meddling. Finally, I worry that one day we will reach the point where we cannot tell if information is accurate or not.

Brendan: What advice would you like to share with the younger generation about technology?

Mary: My advice would be that the younger generation not rely on technology so heavily. It should be an aid, not the entirety of your day. Also, you shouldn’t text or post a comment that is mean, that is something you wouldn’t say to the person’s face, or that is something you wouldn’t want your parents to see. Trust me - when you apply to college or apply to a job, one of the first things that is going to be done is a Google search of you and or a social media search of you. Finally, if you are having a problem with someone or a fight, talk to the person face to face or over the phone. Technology cannot convey emotion and texts can be easily misread when you are upset. Don’t wear headphones in the workplace unless your supervisor tells you it is okay to do so, or you have an accommodation for that.

Brendan: What are your favorite benefits of technology?

Mary: Number 1 would have to be the Starbucks Mobile App. I love being able to order my coffee in advance and then picking it up. Online shopping is very handy, and was very helpful to doing Christmas shopping when you and your brother were young. However, I think there needs to be a balance between online shops and brick and mortar stores.
